UML is a monstrous beast with many heads and countless powers. I generally don't feel comfortable when a complex thing like UML gets marginalized to a simple evidence like "I know UML!".

Recently I tried to find my position towards UML. The copycat I am, I try to summarize the points that are important to me by quoting people hat are much smarter than me:

    * Much of the boosting of UML seems to be based on the statement that pictures are better than text. In cases this is true, but I see no evidence for that in general - anyone who has compared flow charts to pseudo code can form their own conclusions.

    I would really like to see software development raised by a level of abstraction. But I don't see that the UML provides this abstraction jump .
