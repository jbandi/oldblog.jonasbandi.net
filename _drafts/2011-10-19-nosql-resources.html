And some notes from the Yes, SQL! and Enterprise NoSQL presentation:

  • The Relational Model promotes "Model first, query later", this goes hand in hand with the excellent query support in RDBMs
  • NoSQL systems promote the "Query first" / "Question centric" (start with the questions rather than the data) approach. You have to know your queries upfront. Therefore the use-case and not he model is central.
  • Different mindset get obvious by supported operations:
    • SQL operations: Insert, Select, Update, Delete
    • NoSQL Operations: Put, Retrieve by Key, Delete, Search = Table Scan (aka. Map-Reduce)

 

Characteristics of NoSQL systems are:

  • distributed
  • scalable
  • ACID not given, some flavor "eventual consistency" as alternative
  • Normalization is not the highest goal, duplication is perfectly acceptable to reach other goals
  • In NoSQL systems the validation of data/schema is often the responsibility of the application and not of the data store
  • Inconsistencies do happen, you have to deal with them on application level or even business process level


Schema less stores can have different flavors:

  • key-value stores
  • column stores (row oriented, sparse column)
  • document stores
  • network/graph stores
