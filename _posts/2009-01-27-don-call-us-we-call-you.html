--- layout: post title: Don't call us, we call you ... date: '2009-01-27T18:56:00.001+01:00' author: Jonas Bandi tags: - programming - javablog modified_time: '2009-01-27T18:56:44.019+01:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-5763764290649132593.post-6813720612435057604 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.jonasbandi.net/2009/01/don-call-us-we-call-you.html --- This is a very insightful statement from FubuMVC:
Many frameworks are very draconian in their requirements of the application consuming them. They require your application inherit from the framework's base classes, implement their interfaces, etc in order to gain the framework's functionality. In this, more common, style of framework design, the application serves the framework.
That's probably the best subsumption of the ideas behind POJOs as a design concept.
